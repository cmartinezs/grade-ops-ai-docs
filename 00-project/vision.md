# Vision

GradeOps AI helps programming educators run practical assessment workflows with AI agents while preserving teacher control.

## Strategic Thesis

Programming educators do not need another quiz generator, student chatbot, or heavy LMS. They need an operational layer that helps them design, run, grade, review, and improve assessments without multiplying manual work.

GradeOps AI turns assessment work into an AI-assisted operation. Agents draft activities, generate rubrics, analyze submissions, prepare feedback, detect learning gaps, suggest recovery actions, and produce teacher-facing reports.

The teacher remains the final pedagogical authority.

## Initial Wedge

The first market is programming education:

- independent programming teachers;
- tutors;
- bootcamps;
- small academies;
- cohort-based training teams;
- technical instructors who grade practical code submissions.

This wedge is intentionally narrow. Programming assessments are frequent, time-consuming to grade, and rich enough to show real value through code analysis, rubric-based judgment, feedback, and recovery recommendations.

## Long-Term Goal

Become the AI-native operating layer for assessment workflows in small and mid-sized education environments.

The expansion path is not to become a full LMS. The expansion path is to own assessment operations: creation, rubricing, grading assistance, feedback, reporting, recovery planning, evidence, and operational analytics.

## What Success Looks Like

- Teachers recover measurable hours from repetitive assessment work.
- Students receive faster and more actionable feedback.
- Feedback quality becomes more consistent across cohorts.
- Learning gaps are detected earlier.
- Small education teams operate like larger academic teams without increasing staff.
- The business collects clear evidence of usage, value, revenue, and willingness to pay.
- AI agent operations are logged, auditable, and reviewable by humans.

## Non-Negotiables

- Teachers approve important outputs before they affect students.
- Agent decisions are logged.
- The product shows evidence of usage, cost, time saved, and business value.
- The MVP stays focused on programming assessments.
- The project is positioned as a real business operation, not only a software demo.

## Strategic Principle

AI operates the repetitive workflow. Teachers retain judgment, standards, and final approval.
