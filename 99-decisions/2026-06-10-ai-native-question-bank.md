# AI-Native Question Bank

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-06-10
- Decision owner: Product

## Context

In GRADE, the question bank was built primarily through manual teacher authoring. Questions were entered one by one and maintained by academic staff.

The integration analysis (`.raw/13-chat.md`) identified that GradeOps AI's core differentiator is AI-native operations. Applying this to the question bank — making AI the primary generator and the teacher the curator — creates a fundamentally more powerful product than a traditional bank.

## Decision

The GradeOps AI Question Bank is AI-native:

1. Questions are generated by the Question Generation Agent.
2. The system validates structural correctness automatically.
3. Teachers curate, approve, edit, or reject items in a review queue.
4. Only teacher-approved questions can be used in assessments.
5. Teacher can also create questions manually; these follow the same curation flow.

The primary creation flow is:

```text
Teacher defines intent → AI generates batch → Teacher reviews → Approved questions enter bank
```

Not:

```text
Teacher writes each question manually → Enters into bank
```

## Rationale

- AI generation dramatically reduces the time cost of building a useful question bank.
- Curation by the teacher preserves pedagogical quality and trust.
- An AI-native bank is a stronger product differentiator than a manual one.
- The workflow aligns with the broader GradeOps AI principle: AI operates, teacher controls.

## Consequences

- Question bank UX must include a review queue for AI-generated items.
- Questions must carry `source_type` (ai_generated, manual, imported) and `review_status`.
- Approved questions are versioned; edits after approval create a new version.
- Questions used in published assessments are frozen in a snapshot and cannot be retroactively changed.
- The question bank must expose quality flags from Distractor Quality and Ambiguity Review agents.
